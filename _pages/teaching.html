Afra Anam teaches undergraduate Civil Engineering courses, with emphasis on mechanics of solids, structural analysis, ETABS-based structural modeling, and engineering materials. Her teaching focuses on developing students’ conceptual understanding, analytical problem-solving skills, and practical structural modeling ability.

Courses Taught

  • Mechanics of Solids
  • Analysis of Statically Determinate Structures
  • Analysis of Statically Indeterminate Structures
  • ETABS-Based Structural Modeling and Analysis
  • Engineering Materials

Teaching and Academic Activities

Her teaching activities include classroom lectures, problem-solving sessions, laboratory instruction, academic advising, thesis supervision, structural modeling guidance, experimental data interpretation, technical writing, and presentation preparation.

Thesis Supervision

She supervises and assists undergraduate thesis projects related to structural engineering and construction materials. Her supervised areas include seismic assessment of reinforced concrete buildings, structural modeling and analysis, performance evaluation of buildings under lateral loads, sustainable concrete technology, recycled aggregate concrete, high-performance concrete, ultra-high-performance concrete, and supplementary cementitious materials.
